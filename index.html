Creative Services Unification

This structure consolidates all creative production — customer-facing content, marketing and ad assets, learning experience design, and creative operations — into a single creative function. One team, one creative standard, one leader.

Overview

Creative talent at Mindvalley — content production, marketing assets, learning experience design, and operations — is currently spread across multiple functions with inconsistent standards, competing priorities, and no single creative leader accountable for output quality. This structure consolidates that talent into one creative function with clear discipline separation and unified creative direction:

  • Khairul Johari — Film Production (capture + AI-gen)
  • Jia Wen Liu — Post-Production (long-form and short-form content output quality)
  • Titus Thana Raj — Marketing Production (organic + paid editing)
  • Jason Roper — Creative Excellence (direct to Gareth — special projects, quality standards)
  • Matt Wong — Creative Operations (governance, capacity, vendor strategy)

Every creative function — from customer-facing content and marketing assets to learning experience design and creative operations — sits under one creative leader with one set of standards, shared tooling, and a single point of accountability for output quality.

Why This Structure

  • Creative unity: All creative production — customer-facing content, marketing and ad assets, learning experience design — operates under one creative function with shared standards, shared tools, and one creative director setting the bar.
  • Talent consolidation: Creative resources currently distributed across multiple functions are brought together into a single team purpose-built for content production, marketing assets, and learning experience design — eliminating duplication, inconsistency, and competing priorities.
  • Clean discipline separation: Khairul owns production (capture + AI-gen). Jia Wen owns post-production (long-form and short-form output). Titus owns marketing production (social + paid editing). No overlap, no ambiguity.
  • Flex capacity & talent development: With all creative talent under one roof, Gareth can flex resources across priorities — a social editor can support a Quest launch, a VSL editor can cut a campaign asset, an LX designer can support curriculum across formats. Creative Ops (Matt) manages this holistically. Beyond resourcing, a unified function creates real growth pathways: team members collaborate across disciplines, learn from each other's craft, and develop broader creative skill sets. Instead of siloed teams with limited exposure, talent grows inside a single creative culture with mentorship, cross-pollination, and a clear ladder.
  • Quality consistency: Jason Roper reports directly to Gareth as a creative excellence resource — setting and lifting the quality standard across all output, available for high-priority assets and special projects.

Risks & Mitigations

Risk Detail Mitigation
Creative Director availability Gareth is the sole director-level leader across ~22 people and two pillars. If pulled into cross-functional or executive work, day-to-day creative leadership could thin out. Situational deputising: Jason Roper covers creative quality decisions; Khairul Johari covers production operations. Both have direct access to Gareth and can escalate. Matt Wong holds operational continuity through dashboards and process governance.
Marketing Production cultural integration Titus's team comes from a performance-marketing environment (conversion, ROAS, speed). The creative function prioritises craft, quality, and brand consistency. Different instincts could create friction. This is seen as additive, not adversarial. Titus brings a commercial lens that strengthens the creative function's relevance to business outcomes. Shared creative standards, tooling, and regular cross-team reviews will align the culture over time. Jason's creative excellence remit helps set the bar across all output.
Ops capacity as org scales Matt Wong is a single B3 ops specialist covering governance, capacity planning, vendor strategy, and dashboards for the full ~22-person org. No direct reports, no backup. Matt is leveraging AI tooling to multiply his throughput. Gareth will monitor workload and escalate for additional ops support if the function grows beyond current scope. The AI-gen capability across the company also reduces manual ops burden.
Contractor dependency (Film Production) One of Khairul's five team members (Leonardo Govoni) is a contractor. If the contract ends, film production capacity drops by 20%. Khairul maintains a vetted freelancer bench for surge and cover. The flex-capacity model across the unified function means other editors can temporarily support shoots if needed.
Social content strategy ownership This org owns the production and editing of marketing content, but the social strategy layer — what gets made, why, and for whom — sits in Marketing. If Marketing's social strategy function is under-resourced or misaligned, the creative team executes without clear direction. Clear boundary: Marketing owns social strategy; Creative owns execution quality and throughput. Titus is the interface point. Regular planning syncs between Titus and Marketing stakeholders ensure briefs are aligned before production begins. This is a Marketing function gap to solve, not a Creative one.
Knowledge concentration risk Several roles are single-person functions: Jason (creative excellence) and Matt (operations). Losing either creates a significant capability gap. All key roles have documented DNAs, ensuring knowledge is preserved and transferable. The succession table identifies backfill priorities, and the unified structure enables cross-training so adjacent leaders can cover short-term gaps.

Creative Director – Mindvalley Content

Role Mission

Lead the creative vision, production, and strategic impact of Mindvalley's content ecosystem — Quests, meditations, premium products, short-form content, and campaign assets. Partner with the CEO, CPO, CGO, MDM, and senior leadership to ensure every piece of content drives engagement, transformation, and growth.

Architect a content strategy that blends storytelling, product thinking, and AI-native workflows into a single system — moving Mindvalley from launch-driven content cycles to an always-on engine built for deep engagement, habit formation, and long-term retention.

Own the full creative pipeline end to end: from author scouting and curriculum design through on-set direction, post-production, and campaign delivery.

Operate as the creative bridge between Content, Product, and Marketing, ensuring these functions work as one system. Bring creative leadership grounded in data and commercial accountability.

Key Expected Outcomes

  • Strategic Content Leadership: Clear content roadmap aligned to Pathways, with investment criteria for new programmes and lifecycle plans for existing ones.
  • High-Performing Creative Team: Leaders are coached, accountable, and delivering measurable improvements to content quality and production throughput.
  • Customer Impact & Engagement: Content experiences consistently raise completion, re-engagement, and retention through data-informed curation and personalised pathways.
  • Data-Driven & AI-Driven Decision Making: Content priorities guided by consumption analytics, AI-powered pattern recognition, and customer feedback — with post-launch measurement feeding back into the next cycle.
  • AI-Enhanced Creative Engine: AI tools and AI-powered workflows embedded across scriptwriting, campaign generation, and production planning — reducing cycle times and cost per production.
  • Cross-Functional Alignment: Stakeholder consensus on content strategy with smooth delivery across Product, Marketing, and Leadership teams.
  • Business & Financial Accountability: Own and improve key content portfolio metrics (LTV, retention, content-attributed revenue, ROAS), ensuring production investments deliver strong ROI and support sustainable business growth.

Core Responsibilities

  • Set Content & Programme Strategy: Partner with the CPO and senior leadership to define the strategic direction for Mindvalley's content portfolio and translate it into annual roadmaps and quarterly OKRs with clear investment rationale.
  • Own Financial Levers: Align content priorities with business outcomes, building investment cases before greenlighting productions and benchmarking spend against ROAS, LTV, and content-attributed revenue.
  • Lead & Develop Team: Lead a cross-functional team spanning Film Production, Post-Production, Creative Operations, Design, Product Design, Marketing, and Content Coordination. Set clear and ambitious quarterly OKRs, run structured reviews, and build promotion pathways.
  • Own the Content Lifecycle: Guide programmes from author scouting through curriculum design, on-set creative direction, post-production, campaign distribution, and performance review — ensuring every stage has clear ownership, quality standards, and timelines.
  • Drive Data & AI-Led Decisions: Use consumption analytics, behavioural data, AI-powered discovery, and customer feedback to prioritise what to commission, relaunch, or retire. Build scoring frameworks that make decision-making repeatable.
  • Embed AI Across Creative Operations: Deploy AI systems across the creative pipeline — from scriptwriting to campaign generation and gen-AI filmmaking. Build reusable tools the team can operate independently. Partner with engineering to shape platform-level AI capabilities.
  • Set & Protect Creative Standards: Own the storytelling quality, emotional resonance, and brand integrity of everything the content team produces. Direct on set. Review edits. Ensure Mindvalley's content looks, sounds, and feels like the category leader it aspires to be.
  • Cross-Functional Delivery: Operate as the connective layer between Content, Product, Marketing, and Leadership — bringing content insight upstream into strategic conversations and converging on unified plans with clear scope and ownership.

Technical & Industry Skills

  • Proven ability to set creative vision and translate it into portfolio-level strategy, annual roadmaps, and quarterly execution across multiple formats and channels.
  • Expertise in storytelling, scriptwriting, and narrative craft — shaping author stories into structured, emotionally compelling learning experiences.
  • On-set creative direction — directing authors, presenters, and production teams under live production pressure.
  • Strong command of subscription and retention mechanics within digital membership models.
  • Skilled in building and deploying AI-native creative systems across the production pipeline.
  • Commercial and data fluency — connecting creative decisions to business metrics, building investment cases, and reading consumption analytics.
  • Experience in brand building, positioning, and evolving established brands into new categories.

Soft Skills

  • Creative Judgement: Ability to assess content quality across scripts, edits, and campaigns — setting and holding the bar without micromanaging execution.
  • Strategic Influence: Gains alignment from senior leadership through evidence-backed cases and clear storytelling. Comfortable presenting to founders and C-suite.
  • Translation Between Creative & Commercial: Speaks fluently to both storytellers and commercial leaders. Turns data into briefs and creative ambition into business cases.
  • Resilience Under Ambiguity: Absorbs strategic redirects, competing priorities, and shifting timelines without losing momentum. Re-architects plans quickly when the landscape changes.
  • People Development: Builds individuals, not just teams. Identifies potential, coaches through honest feedback, and invests in growth.
  • Technical Curiosity: Learns by building. Picks up new tools and platforms on real problems. Leads AI adoption with credibility, not just enthusiasm.

Senior Creative Operations Specialist – Mindvalley Creative

Department: Creative
Reports to: Creative Director
Level: B3
Effective Date: 1 April 2026 (target)

Role Mission

Own the operational performance, governance, and continuous improvement of Mindvalley's creative function. Ensure that creative teams across Film Production, Post-Production, Design, Product Design, Social Media, Marketing, Content Coordination, and Learning Experience deliver on time, within budget, and with full visibility into capacity, cost, and workflow health.

Operate at the intersection of creative teams, senior leadership, and cross-functional stakeholders, translating business priorities into operational execution, building systems, and owning the business outcomes those systems produce.

Key Expected Outcomes

  • Operational Governance & Performance: SLAs established and enforced across all creative teams. Delivery turnaround, cost per deliverable, and team utilisation tracked, and actioned, optimised continuously, in addition to reporting.
  • Cross-Functional Coordination: Operational interface between creative teams and the wider business runs smoothly. Scheduling, resourcing, and process discipline maintained across all key meetings, campaigns, productions and launch cycles.
  • Capacity Planning & Resource Forecasting: Department-wide capacity picture owned holistically across all creative functions (Content, Films, Design, Social Media, Events, Marketing), with accurate forecasts and proactive bottleneck resolution.
  • Vendor & Budget Strategy: Accountability for creative operations spend. Annual vendor strategy, contract negotiations, and cost optimisation managed with a utilisation-first approach.
  • Process Improvement & Automation: Strategic automation roadmap prioritised against business objectives, delivering measurable reductions in cycle time and cost per deliverable.
  • Data, Reporting & Operational Intelligence: Operational dashboards provide real-time department-wide visibility. OKRs are owned, not just tracked, with escalations and action plans driven when metrics move off track.
  • Launch Planning & Campaign Coordination: Large-scale campaigns and launches operationally locked in ahead of execution, with cross-team alignment and minimal bottlenecks.

Core Responsibilities

  • Own Operational Governance: Establish and enforce SLAs, monitor delivery turnaround times, manage archive and storage, and report on operational health to senior leadership.
  • Lead Cross-Functional Coordination: Serve as the operational interface between content, creative teams and product, marketing, programmes, and executive leadership. Maintain active presence in weekly coordination and planning meetings.
  • Own Capacity Planning: Maintain a real-time view of team bandwidth across all creative functions: Films, Post-Production, Design, Product Design, Social Media, Marketing, and Learning Experience. Proactively identify bottlenecks and provide leadership with accurate resource forecasts.
  • Manage Vendor & Budget Strategy: Own annual vendor and tooling strategy, including budgeting, forecasting, renewal cycles, contract negotiations, and proactive cost optimisation. Apply a utilisation-first approach to every vendor and subscription decision.
  • Drive Process Improvement & Automation: Own and prioritise a formal automation roadmap leveraging Airtable, Jira Automation, Claude and Make.com. Sequence improvements against business objectives and report on progress regularly.
